Nowadays

I'm a Research Fellow at The University of Nottingham/ King's group where I generate and characterize durum wheat material with alien introgression using molecular techniques: KASP markers, cytogenetic analysis: mcGISH and FISH and phenotypic data collected in different environments: Mexico (CIMMYT), Morroco (ICARDA), India (IIWBR) and Malawi (LUANAR).

My PhD road

For my thesis entitled: "Genetic analysis of physiological traits to increase grain partitioning in high biomass cultivars in wheat (Triticum aestivum L.)". The format is in series of articles that will be submitted to high-impact journals. Each chapter will be composed of an introduction (including hypothesis for each specific section), material and methods, results, discussion and conclusions. MY THESIS The framework includes seven chapters from which one will cover a wide range of themes wheat related (introduction), three will be dedicated to explain physiological trait results obtained during seasons 2015-16 and 2016-17 from the HiBAP (experiment sown in Obregon, Sonora, Mexico) and SxR DH (experiment sown in Suttong Bonnington, Loughborough, United Kindgdom) and as well for season 2017-18 a 'little' experiment with 12 cultivars was sown to tested high biomass genotypes under PGR conditions, two chapters will report novel findings of the GWAS and QTL analysis and in the last chapter results from the overall project will be reviewed.
